About Our Church

Full Gospel Churches of Kenya–Malindi Local Church Assembly is a Faith Based Organization (FBO) located in Malindi Town, Kilifi County in Kenya. The church started its operations on 14th June 1986 and currently has about 270 members from diverse communities, backgrounds, and professions.

The church exists to live and collaborate with communities to transform and improve the lives of people through the love and teachings of Jesus Christ. We believe in accomplishing the Great Commission of Christ as stated in Matthew 28:19 and reaching the world with the love of Christ.

Challenges Facing the Church

To accomplish its mission, the church must address several challenges. These include knowledge and education gaps, low membership, obsolete infrastructure, inadequate facilities, limited access to water and sanitation, and limited land for expansion. The church also faces financial limitations that affect mission work, community outreach programs, staff salaries, and general operations.

Opportunities and Strengths

Despite these challenges, the church has strong opportunities to positively impact the church and the wider community. It has a wide catchment area to reach many people with the gospel and community services. The church also has a strong leadership team, unity among members, a vibrant congregation, and goodwill from local community leaders.

The church works to support vulnerable members of society including people living with disabilities, widows, widowers, and orphans while addressing gender inequalities in the community.

Strategic Plan

The church has developed a 7-Year Strategic Plan to guide its future growth and development. The plan focuses on strengthening the church financially, acquiring land for expansion, improving infrastructure, increasing membership, and empowering communities through spiritual and social programs.

The estimated budget required to implement the strategic plan is KES 42,000,000. Successful implementation will result in a stronger church organization, empowered communities, stronger families, and reduced crime rates in the surrounding communities.

Our Objectives

  • To promote the spiritual welfare and growth of believers.

  • To establish new church branches that can later become self-supporting local churches.

  • To develop adequate church infrastructure.
